FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Honest answers for schools who’ve been burned by complicated software before.

We already tried school software. It didn't work. Why is this different?

Most school software dumps 50 features on you and expects your staff to learn menus. Mentivax is built around simplicity — type what you need in plain words and get it done. No training manuals, no clicking through 10 screens. And you only pay for what you actually use.

We already use school software. Do we have to replace it?

No. Mentivax works alongside your existing software — you don't have to switch or replace anything. If your current system handles some things well, keep using it. Mentivax fills the gaps — the tasks your staff still does manually, the reports that take hours, the reminders nobody sends on time. Zero disruption.

We're a small school with a tight budget. Can we afford this?

Mentivax is built for small and medium schools, not big city chains. You don't pay for features you don't need. Start with just fee management, and add more only when you're ready. We'll work within your budget — we'd rather help 100 small schools than 5 big ones.

Our staff is not tech-savvy. Will they be able to use it?

If they can type a WhatsApp message, they can use Mentivax. There are no menus to learn, no complex screens. Just type your question — "how much fee is pending for Class 6?" — and get the answer. It's that simple.

What happens to our old Excel files and registers?

We import everything for you — fees, student records, marks, whatever you have. You don't re-type anything. Your old data is cleaned up and ready from day one.

Do parents need to install an app?

No. Parents receive everything — fee reminders, results, receipts, notices — directly on WhatsApp. No app download needed. Works on any phone, even basic smartphones.

Is our school data safe?

Yes. Your data is encrypted and backed up daily. Only your authorised staff can access it. It's far safer than an Excel file on someone's laptop that could crash, get deleted, or be taken when they leave.
